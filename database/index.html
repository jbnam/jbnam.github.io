Let $\mathcal B_k$ be the family of primitive Dirichlet characters of order $k$ and define

$$\mathcal{B}_{k,N}(X) = \{\chi \in \mathcal B_k \mid \mathfrak{f}_\chi \leq X \text{ and } \text{gcd}(N, \mathfrak{f}_\chi)=1\}$$

where $\text{gcd}$ is the greatest common divisor function and $\mathfrak{f}_\chi$ is the conductor of $\chi$.

Each list of a data file consists of the following 11 entries:

$$\big[ N, k, \mathfrak{f}_\chi, r_\chi, L(E,1,\chi), \tau(\chi), e_\chi(N), c, e_\chi(c), e_\chi(-1), T_\chi \big]$$

where

  • $N$ - The conductor of an elliptic curve $E$ defined over $\mathbb{Q}$
  • $k$ - The order of a primitive Dirichlet character $\chi$
  • $\mathfrak{f}_\chi$ - The conductor of $\chi$
  • $r_\chi$ - The label of $\chi$ defined as below
  • $L(E,1,\chi)$ - The central value of $L(E, s, \chi)$
  • $\tau(\chi)$ - The Gauss sum of $\chi$
  • $e_\chi(N)$ -
  • $c$ - The least positive integer with $\chi(c)$ is a primitive $k$-th root of unity
  • $e_\chi(c)$ -
  • $e_\chi(-1)$ -
  • $T_\chi$ - The number of terms computed for the value $L(E,1,\chi)$ within our desired precision

To see the whole data at once, go to my cloud storage directory in Microsoft OneDrive. In that directory, each sobj file name has the format: x_y_z_raw.sobj where

  • x - The Cremona label of $E$
  • y - $k$
  • z - 3m (or 1m) for $3\cdot 10^6$ (or $10^6$) repectively
